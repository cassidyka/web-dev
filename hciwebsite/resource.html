Usability Heuristics

#1 Visibility of System Status

As a designer you need to make sure that the user knows what is going on with the system at the current time and the user should be given some form of appropriate feedback that allows them to understand the current status of the system.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#2 Match Between the System and the Real-World

The system's design should be sure to speak the same language as its users. Users should see familiar phrases, icons, images to that of the real-world. This allows a natural, logical and familiar understanding to the aspects of your design for whoever is using it.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#3 User Control and Freedom

Sometimes users up somewhere they shouldn't, or don't want to be. Your design needs to give users the feeling of control and that they have the ability to navigate the design at their own will such as fixing mistakes, correcting choices and finding a simple way to return to where they previous were.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#4 Consistency and Standards

Consistency provides users with the familiarity, patterns and logic for the user. It is about adhering to the conventions of your design and making sure that your design is consistent in itself as well as aligning itself with all other designs that are similar to yours. Both internal and external consistency contribute to learnability and usability of your designs.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#5 Error Prevention

Error prevention is about all the safety nets, notices, and pre-emptive warnings that a user is given in order to steer them into the right direction for how to use or do whatever it is they need to do. Error prevention are all the things that occur before an error can be made and everything that is put in place to avoid errors before they start.
Be careful to not confuse this for error recovery which only applies after a mistake has been make.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#6 Recognition rather than Recall

Users show be able to regocnise aspects of your design, rather than having to recall them from scratch. These aspects should be visible and easily accessible, and allow the user to be able to track their previous uses, understand actions they need to take recognise how to navigate their way through your design.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#7 Flexibility and Efficiency of Use

Catering to your more experienced and informed users is an important way of using your design. your design should be able to cater for the novice user and the experienced user. Catering to the experienced user is about shortcuts that may be unseen to the novice user, allowing users to take multiple approaches to the same task. These users need to be able to take advantage of accelerators or secondary features that allow them to use your design more efficiently and quickly.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#8 Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

Visually appealing designs are basically the entire point of a design. But what can you do to create an aesthetic regardless of your creative ability? Clutter, too much in one spot creates a bad aesthetic, but minimising the things on your design, and focussing on what is important and how to make the few select elements be aesthetic is key. Using design principles such as C.R.A.P. is a great place to start in terms of creating an aesthetic. It is a combination of how all the elements of your design fit together and complement each other from colour, to sizing, shapes and placement, they must all work together.

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#9 Error Recovery

Using will always make a mistake, no matter how much you try to steer them in the right direction, so you need to make sure that you have processes for helping users understand that not only have they made a mistake, but how they should try and fix that mistake the next time. There needs to be clear indication of what has happened, why and what to do next. This heuristic is only applicable after a mistake has occurred, not before. Remember the difference between a recovery and reactive element as opposed to a preventative one!

Click on the resources below for more information

Article from the NN group Article from Medium - Harsh Gorasia
#10 Help and Documentation

Every design should be able to help users get familiar with the interface and well as provide help for troubleshooting any issues the user might run into. It is helpful to provide the user with some form of documentation that allows users to find specific information to fix a problem the user might have. It is also to provide users with help at the beginning to teach them how to interact with your design.

Bootstrap

Bootstrap is a free, third-party HTML, CSS and JS library that is built for easy-to-use responsive website development. When using Bootstrap make sure to link the appropriate versions of CSS and JS so that all of the built-in features and animations work. Alternatively, You can download the Bootstrap library and the SASS files to more directly access and edit the library and CSS classes.
